If you read one lesson in this whole guide and act on it, make it this one. Ingress is not really a solo game, and the agents who quit early are almost always the ones who tried to play it alone.

## Why this matters more than mechanics

You can learn to hack, deploy, link, and field from this guide. What this guide cannot give you is the local knowledge that makes the game click: which portals farm well, when the next event is, who to ask when you are stuck, and the dozens of small things your local team already knows. That comes from people, not pages.

The agents in your area can also do things for you that the game cannot. They can tank high-level portals so a level 1 agent can still take down a level 8. They can hand you gear. They can tell you where to be on Saturday. Get connected and the game opens up. Stay disconnected and it stays small and confusing.

## How agents actually talk

There are two layers to communication in Ingress.

**In-scanner comms.** Your scanner has a chat, called **comms**, with two channels: **faction** (only your team sees it) and **all** (everyone in range sees it, both factions). Faction comms is where you can call for help, ask a question, or get recruited deeper into your local team. To message a specific agent, tag their name with an `@` in front of it.

A word of caution: anything you post in **all** is visible to the opposition. Do not announce your plans there. "Farming the post office at 7" in all comms is an invitation for green to come and ruin your evening.

**Off-scanner chat.** This is where the real community lives, and it varies by region. Most local groups run on **Telegram**, some on **Signal**, **Discord**, or other platforms. There is no single official channel, which is deliberate. Each area sorts out its own.

The way to find yours is simple: **ask in faction comms.** Post something like "new RES agent in [your area], where's the local chat?" and someone will point you in the right direction. If the person who sent you this link is local, ask them directly. That is the fastest path of all.

## Just reach out

New agents often hesitate to message strangers. Do not. Every experienced agent was a confused level 1 once, and local teams are almost always glad to see a new face on their side. The worst case is someone is slow to reply. The best case is you have just found the people who will make this game one of the better decisions you have made.
