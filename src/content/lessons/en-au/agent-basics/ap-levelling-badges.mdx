**AP** (Access Points) is your experience. You earn it by doing useful things, and it's what levels you up. There are 16 levels, and they work in two distinct phases.

## Earning AP

Almost every constructive action pays AP. The big earners:

| Action | AP |
|---|---|
| Capture a portal (first resonator) | 500 |
| Deploy a resonator | 125 |
| Complete a portal (8th resonator) | 250 |
| Create a link | 313 |
| Create a control field | 1,250 |
| Deploy a mod | 125 |
| Destroy an enemy resonator | 75 |
| Destroy an enemy link | 187 |
| Destroy an enemy control field | 750 |
| Hack an enemy portal | 100 |

Glyph hacking pays bonus AP on top, which is part of why it's worth learning. The pattern to notice: **building and linking pays far more than destroying.** Capturing a portal, deploying all eight resonators, then linking and fielding it nets thousands of AP. That's the efficient way up.

Your level caps the level of gear you can use. A level 3 agent can't fire a level 8 burster or deploy a level 8 resonator. So **reaching level 8 is your first real goal**, because at level 8 you can use every item in the game and become genuinely useful to your team. Until then, focus on capturing and building everything in reach. Team up with a higher-level agent who clears enemy portals for you to capture, and you'll both rack up AP fast.

## Levels 9 to 16: the long game

Past level 8, AP alone isn't enough. You also need **medals** (badges) at specific tiers:

{/* VISUAL: the five medal tiers (bronze, silver, gold, platinum, onyx) as example badge art */}

| Level | AP | Medals |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | 2,400,000 | 1 <Medal tier="gold" />, 4 <Medal tier="silver" /> |
| 10 | 4,000,000 | 2 <Medal tier="gold" />, 5 <Medal tier="silver" /> |
| 11 | 6,000,000 | 4 <Medal tier="gold" />, 6 <Medal tier="silver" /> |
| 12 | 8,400,000 | 6 <Medal tier="gold" />, 7 <Medal tier="silver" /> |
| 13 | 12,000,000 | 1 <Medal tier="platinum" />, 7 <Medal tier="gold" /> |
| 14 | 17,000,000 | 2 <Medal tier="platinum" />, 7 <Medal tier="gold" /> |
| 15 | 24,000,000 | 3 <Medal tier="platinum" />, 7 <Medal tier="gold" /> |
| 16 | 40,000,000 | 2 <Medal tier="onyx" />, 4 <Medal tier="platinum" />, 7 <Medal tier="gold" /> |

One thing that makes this less daunting than it looks: **medals count cumulatively.** A <Medal tier="gold">Gold</Medal> medal also counts as a <Medal tier="silver">Silver</Medal> and a <Medal tier="bronze">Bronze</Medal> of the same type. So if a level needs "2 Gold, 5 Silver", your two Golds already cover two of the five Silvers, and you only need three more Silver-tier medals from elsewhere.

Levels 9 and up don't unlock new gear, since you can already use everything at level 8. What they do give you is more **XM capacity** and standing in your community. They're a mark of how much you've played, not a power gate.

## The short version

Sprint to level 8 by capturing and building everything you can. After that, keep playing the way you enjoy, and the badges and higher levels come on their own.
