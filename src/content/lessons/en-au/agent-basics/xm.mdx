**XM**, or Exotic Matter, is the energy that runs everything. In the story it's a mysterious force the factions are fighting over. In practice it's the fuel you spend to take actions, and you'll be managing it constantly.

## Collecting it

XM appears as drifting white and blue specks scattered across the map, clustered most thickly around portals. You collect it the lazy way: by **walking over it**. As you move, your scanner hoovers up any XM you pass through and tops up your bar. You don't tap anything. Just moving through the world fills you up.

This is why standing still in a dead spot is a bad idea, and why a quick lap around a cluster of portals refills you nicely.

## Your XM bar

Your current XM sits in a bar near the top of your scanner. It has a maximum capacity, and that capacity **grows as you level up**, so a high-level agent can hold and spend far more before needing a refill. When the bar hits zero, you can't deploy, attack, or recharge until you collect more.

## What it powers

XM gets spent on the active things you do:

- **Deploying** resonators and mods onto a portal.
- **Attacking**, every XMP burster or Ultra Strike you fire.
- **Recharging** portals to keep them healthy.

Notably, **hacking doesn't cost XM.** Hacking is how you get gear, and it's free, which is handy because it means you can always farm items even when your bar is low.

## When you run dry in the field

Sometimes you'll be mid-battle, far from a good XM cluster, and your bar empties. That's what **power cubes** are for: stored XM you can tap to instantly refill. You hack them out of portals and save them for exactly these moments. There's a whole lesson on them later in the gear op. For now, just know that running out of XM isn't the end of the world, and that moving is the simplest way to refuel.
