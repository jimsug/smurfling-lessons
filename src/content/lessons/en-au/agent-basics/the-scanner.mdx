The Ingress app is called your **scanner**, and the story reason is that you're scanning the world for XM and portals. Practically, it's the screen you'll be staring at, so let's get you oriented. The exact layout shifts a little between updates, but these elements are always there.

## The map

Most of the screen is a stylised map of the world around you, centred on your position. Your agent is the arrow in the middle. Around you is a glowing ring: that's your **range**, the area you can reach to interact with things. To hack, deploy, or attack a portal, the portal needs to be inside that ring, which in practice means getting within about 40 metres of it.

Scattered across the map you'll see **portals** (the tall glowing markers) and the drifting specks of **XM**. More on both shortly.

## Your stats

Somewhere near the top you'll find your **agent name**, your **level**, and your **AP** (your progress toward the next level). Tucked alongside is your **XM bar**, which shows how much XM you currently have to spend on actions. Keep half an eye on it. When it's empty, you can't do much.

## The menus you'll actually use

- **Inventory** (often labelled OPS): everything you're carrying. Resonators, weapons, keys, mods, power cubes. You'll be in here constantly.
- **Comms**: the in-game chat. Two channels, faction and all. This is covered properly back in the community lesson, but know where it lives.
- **Main menu**: gets you to your agent profile, scores, settings, and the other screens you'll need less often.

## Tapping a portal

Tap any portal on the map and its info comes up: its name, who owns it, its level, and what you can do with it. If you're in range, the action buttons light up: **hack**, **deploy**, **link**, and so on. If they're greyed out, you're too far away. Walk closer.

That's the whole interface in a nutshell. Don't worry about memorising it. Five minutes of poking around in the field will teach you more than this page can, so get outside and tap things.
