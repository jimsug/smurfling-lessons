**Resonators** are what you deploy to claim and power up a portal. Capturing a grey portal is as simple as deploying one resonator on it. Building a portal up means filling all its slots.

## The basics

Every portal has **eight resonator slots**. Resonators come in levels 1 to 8, and the portal's level is the average of whatever's in those eight slots. To deploy, get in range, tap the portal, and use the deploy option. On a neutral portal, your first resonator captures it for the Resistance.

## Deployment limits

You can't stack a portal with eight level 8 resonators by yourself. Each agent is capped on how many of each resonator level they can put on a single portal:

Work it through and the best a single agent can manage is a **level 5 portal**. Anything higher needs more agents stacking their resonators on the same portal. This is by design: high-level portals are meant to be a team effort.

## Placement actually matters

Here's the bit new agents skip, then regret. When you deploy, you can choose **where** each resonator sits around the portal by tapping a slot before deploying.

If you stand right on top of the portal and deploy everything, all eight resonators cluster together at the base. This is called a **campfired** portal, and it's weak: a single well-aimed enemy Ultra Strike can hit the whole cluster at once. Like rocks around a campfire, they're all in one spot.

Instead, deploy from the **edge of your range**. This spreads the resonators out around the portal, so an attacker has to work harder to hit them all. And where you can, put your **higher-level resonators on the far side**, away from the road or the obvious approach, so they survive longer when the portal gets attacked.

It's a small habit that makes your portals meaningfully tougher. Build them properly and your team will thank you. Campfire them and they'll quietly redo your work.
