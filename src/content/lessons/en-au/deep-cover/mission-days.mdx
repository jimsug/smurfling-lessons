A **Mission Day** is a gentler kind of event than an anomaly. Instead of a faction battle, it's about **exploration**: a city lays out a set of missions (more on missions in a later lesson), and agents spend the day completing them, earning a Mission Day badge and seeing parts of a place they'd never otherwise visit.

## What they're like

Mission Days are **cross-faction** and unhurried. You walk a route, complete the missions in whatever order suits, and usually end up with a nice piece of **banner artwork** on your profile for your trouble. They tend to take you through the interesting bits of a city: the laneways, the public art, the history, exactly the kind of discovery the game is built around.

They're often held the **day after an anomaly** (the Sunday following a Saturday anomaly), so agents who've travelled for the big event get a relaxed second day out of the trip.

## A note on their current status

Worth knowing, because it's recent: when Ingress moved to Niantic Spatial in 2025, the team got much smaller, and **standalone Mission Days were paused** for a while. They've since returned, and they now lean more on **local agents to host them**, so the Mission Days running in your region are likely organised by your own community rather than centrally.

What this means for you: if there's a Mission Day near you, it's probably a local effort worth supporting, and if your community is keen, agents can apply to host one. Check the Ingress events page and your local chat for what's happening.

## Why bother

Mission Days are low-pressure and high-reward for a new agent. You get **unique portal visits and captures** (good for badges), a banner for your profile, a proper walk around somewhere interesting, and a relaxed day among other agents with none of the intensity of an anomaly. If you want to dip a toe into the events scene before committing to a full anomaly weekend, a Mission Day is the perfect place to start.
