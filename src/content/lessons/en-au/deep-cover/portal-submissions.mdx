Ever wonder where portals come from? Agents submit them. Once you're a high enough level, you can nominate real-world places to become portals, which is one of the most lasting contributions you can make, especially if your area is short on them.

{/* VISUAL: the Wayfarer nomination screen, photo plus title and description */}

## How it works now

New portal nominations are reviewed through **Niantic Wayfarer**, the system that long ago replaced the old OPR (Operation Portal Recon). If you see a guide talking about OPR, that's the outdated name for the same idea.

To nominate a portal:

- You need to be **level 8 or higher**. (This dropped from level 10 in early 2025, so older guides will say 10.)
- Use **Nominations** from the Ops menu, within about 25km of the spot.
- You provide a **photo** of the candidate, a **supporting photo** showing the surroundings (so reviewers can see it's safe and accessible), a **title**, a **description**, and a short statement of **why it should be a portal**.
- You can have up to **40 nominations** in progress at once.

Other agents then review your nomination on Wayfarer and vote on whether it makes the grade.

## What makes a good portal

Strong candidates are places with **a story, history, or educational value**: public art, interesting architecture, monuments, plaques, community gathering spots, hidden local gems. Weak or rejected ones include **private residences, natural features, anything you can't safely walk up to, temporary or seasonal installations**, and anything that would put agents in the way of emergency services.

## Important: it's currently paused

Worth flagging clearly, because it's a recent change: **portal nominations were paused in early 2026**, and at the time of writing there's no announced date for them resuming. So you may find the Nominations option unavailable right now. This kind of thing changes, so check the Ingress news and your community for the current status before assuming you can or can't submit.

## Why it matters

When nominations are open, submitting good portals is genuinely valuable work. A portal-sparse area becomes playable when someone takes the time to nominate the local landmarks, and those portals outlast any single operation. If you've got an eye for the interesting corners of your neighbourhood, this is a quietly satisfying way to leave your mark on the map, for your whole community, long term.
