**Dispatch** is a system built into your scanner that delivers structured events as **Campaigns**. It's relatively new, so older guides won't mention it at all, but it's now how a lot of Ingress's recurring and seasonal content reaches you.

{/* VISUAL: the Dispatch interface showing a campaign and its bounties */}

## How it works

A Campaign is made up of **Bounties**, which are objectives to complete (think "capture this many portals" or "create this many links"). Work through the Bounties, claim the associated **Assignment rewards**, and you earn gear, AP, and often a medal. Some Campaigns are free; some premium ones can be claimed or purchased.

The key thing is just to **check the Dispatch interface regularly**. There's frequently something running that hands out free rewards for doing things you'd be doing anyway.

## What it powers

Dispatch is the engine behind a growing list of events:

- **Second Sunday (#Ingress2S).** The monthly low-key counterpart to First Saturday. Instead of an in-person gathering, you complete at least one Bounty in the Second Sunday Campaign and claim the rewards to progress a **Second Sunday medal**. Because it runs through Dispatch, you can take part on your own time and from wherever you are, which makes it a friendly, no-pressure event for a new agent.
- **Global Ops and Campaigns**, larger worldwide objectives often tied to the current anomaly season.
- **Bounty Blitz** and **Daily Assignments**, smaller recurring tasks and rewards.
- **Virtual NL-1331 tours**, as mentioned in the last lesson.

## Why it's worth your attention

For a new agent, Dispatch is close to free progression. The Bounties usually reward ordinary play (hacking, capturing, linking), so you complete them just by getting out and playing, and you pick up gear and medals on the way. It also gives you a steady sense of "something to work toward" between the bigger events.

Make a habit of opening Dispatch when you start a session. See what's live, grab anything easy, and let your normal play tick the boxes. It's one of the simplest ways to get more out of the time you're already spending in the field.
