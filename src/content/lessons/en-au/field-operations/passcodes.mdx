**Passcodes** are codes you redeem for free gear: items, a bit of XM, and some AP. They're a nice top-up, especially early on when good gear is hard to come by.

## How to redeem them

This is the one place your phone matters:

- **Android:** redeem in the scanner. There's a passcode field in the menu and at the bottom of the store. Enter the code, submit, done.
- **iPhone / iOS:** the in-scanner option is disabled, so you redeem on the **Intel Map** instead. Go to intel.ingress.com, use the **Passcode** link in the upper area of the page, and enter it there.

Either way, a code works **once per agent**. After you've redeemed one, it's used up for you, though the same code still works for everyone who hasn't used it yet.

## Things worth knowing

- **One use each.** Don't bother trying a code twice. "Passcode already redeemed" just means you've had it.
- **Don't spam them.** Enter too many too quickly and you'll get a "circuitry too hot" cooldown, roughly half an hour, before you can enter another.
- **Mind your inventory.** Redemption is blocked when you're at the 2,000-item cap, but a failed attempt doesn't burn the code. Recycle or drop something to get under the cap and redeem it again. You only need to be under the cap to *start*: once it goes through, you get the whole reward even if that pushes you back over 2,000.
- **Forever vs limited.** Some codes can be redeemed by everyone indefinitely; others are limited and eventually run dry. If a code you've never used says "fully redeemed", it's just expired.

## Where to find them

Codes turn up in Ingress announcements, at events (First Saturday often has one), and in community channels. There are also maintained lists of long-lived "forever" codes online; search for an Ingress passcode list and you'll find current ones. As ever, your local chat is a good source, since codes handed out at local events sometimes circulate there first.

A small habit: when you pick up a batch of codes, redeem them when your inventory has room rather than when it's stuffed full, or you'll waste the good ones on a failed redemption.
