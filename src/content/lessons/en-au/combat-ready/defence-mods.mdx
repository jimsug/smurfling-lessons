Capturing a portal is only half the job. Keeping it blue is the other half, and that's what mods are for. Every portal has **four mod slots**, and each agent can fit **two mods per portal**, so a fully modded portal usually takes a couple of you working together.

Mods come in rarities, and rarer is both stronger and **stickier** (harder for an attacker to knock off). Deploying any mod earns 125 AP.

## Shields

The **Portal Shield** is your bread and butter. It adds **mitigation**, which reduces the damage the portal's resonators take when attacked. The rarer the shield, the more it adds:

| Shield | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Common | 30 |
| Rare | 40 |
| Very Rare | 60 |
| Aegis | 70 (toughest to strip) |

Two things to understand about mitigation:

- It **caps at 95%.** You can't make a portal immune. Stack shields past the cap and the extra is wasted, though a spare shield does help if an attacker strips one off.
- **Links also add mitigation.** A portal with several links already has meaningful mitigation before you add a single shield, so you often don't need to fill every slot with shields.

The catch: an attacker with **Ultra Strikes** can peel shields off, so shields buy time rather than guarantee safety. That time lets your team recharge the portal remotely and react.

## Turrets and force amps

These are the aggressive mods. Instead of protecting the portal, they punish anyone attacking it by **draining the attacker's XM**.

- **Force Amp** multiplies the damage of the portal's zap. One roughly doubles it. Stacking more has steep diminishing returns (a second only takes it to about 2.5x), so one is usually plenty.
- **Turret** makes the portal zap **more often** and hit harder.

Together, on a shielded portal, these force an attacker to burn power cubes just to stay standing. They're especially nasty on portals along a known enemy commute, where an attacker has little time to spare. Both are Rare and not very sticky, so expect to replace them after a fight.

## The practical takeaway

For a new agent, **shields are the priority**. Throwing a shield or two on portals you capture, especially ones your team cares about, is one of the most useful habits you can build. Turrets and force amps are situational tools you'll use more as you get into defending key portals. And the single best defence of all isn't a mod at all: it's a portal that's well linked and has agents nearby willing to recharge it.
