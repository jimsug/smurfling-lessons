The **flip cards** are two of the rarest and most dramatic items in the game. They instantly switch a portal's faction, no resonators to destroy, no fight. There are two:

- **ADA Refactor** flips an **Enlightened (green) portal to Resistance (blue)**.
- **JARVIS Virus** flips a **Resistance (blue) portal to Enlightened (green)**.

Both are Very Rare, and **either faction can carry and use either card**. A Resistance agent can hold a JARVIS Virus, and there are good reasons to, which we'll get to.

{/* VISUAL: a portal flipping faction (green to blue) with its links dropping away */}

## What happens when you use one

You need the target portal within range (40m). Using a card has some specific effects:

- If you flip a portal **to your own faction**, you gain ownership of the portal and **all its resonators and mods**. This is the one way to end up "owning" more resonators on a portal than the normal deploy limits allow, which has clever uses.
- If you flip a portal **to the opposite faction** (for example, a Resistance agent using a JARVIS on a blue portal), ownership goes to a non-player account, `__ADA__` or `__JARVIS__`, not to you.
- **All links and fields** attached to the portal are destroyed.
- You get **no AP**, and it **doesn't count as a capture** in your stats.
- On a **Machina (red)** portal, a flip card just neutralises it.

## The costs and limits

- It costs **1,000 XM per portal level** (so 8,000 XM for a level 8). Because your scanner won't let you drain fully to zero, you effectively **can't flip a portal more than about one level above your own**.
- After any flip, the portal is **immune to flip cards for one hour**. Use a card on an immune portal and you get "Failed. Portal Temporarily Immune", and **the card is wasted**. Unlike a failed passcode, a wasted flip card is gone, so check the portal hasn't been flipped recently.

## Why a Resistance agent carries a JARVIS

It seems backwards to hold the enemy's card, but flip cards have a sharp tactical use: **breaking your own links**. Normally the only way to remove a friendly link is to wait for the portal to decay or be attacked. A flip card lets you flip one of your own portals to instantly drop every link attached to it, clearing a lane that's blocking a field you want to build.

They can also be used to **inoculate** a key portal: flip it, and it's immune to being flipped back for an hour, which can protect it at a crucial moment.

These are advanced, situational plays you won't need early on. For now, just know what the cards are, that they're precious, and that the red flips blue and the blue flips green. When you find your first one, ask your team before spending it, because there's often a smarter use than the obvious one.
