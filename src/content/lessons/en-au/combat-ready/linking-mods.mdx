Most of the time, a portal's natural link range is plenty. But when you want to reach a portal that's just too far, or throw more than eight links from a single anchor, you need linking mods. There are two kinds.

## Link Amps

A **link amp** extends how far a portal can link out. They come in two rarities:

| Link amp | Range boost |
|---|---|
| Rare | 2x |
| Very Rare | 7x |

A Very Rare link amp turns an ordinary portal into one that can reach a very long way, which is how agents make those huge cross-region links you see on the Intel Map. You can't hack Very Rare Link Amps.

If you stack multiple link amps, only the **rarest gives its full boost**; the next is a quarter as effective, and any beyond that less still. So one good link amp is usually the play, not four.

## SoftBank Ultra Link (SBUL)

The **SoftBank Ultra Link**, usually just called an **SBUL** or **ultra link**, is a special Very Rare linking mod, and unlike the Very Rare link amp it can be **hacked from portals** rather than only found rarely. It does three things at once:

- Extends link range by **5x**.
- Adds **8 more outbound links**, on top of the usual eight, per SBUL. This is how anchor portals end up throwing dozens of links.
- **Boosts the mitigation** your links provide (by 1.5x). Note it doesn't add mitigation on its own, it makes each existing link's mitigation count for more, so it only helps a portal that actually has links.

SBULs are the workhorse mod for big fielding operations, where one anchor portal needs to throw a huge number of links to layer fields over an area.

## Do you need these yet?

Not for everyday play. Rare Link Amps will almost never be useful - you can recycle these for space. Most linking and fielding you'll do as a new agent works fine on a portal's natural range. Linking mods come into their own for **long links and big operations**, which tend to be planned team efforts. Hang on to any you find, and you'll be glad of them when your team plans something ambitious.
