Portals are the whole game. Everything you do happens at one. They're real-world points of interest, the landmarks, murals, statues, post offices, and odd bits of public art that someone, somewhere, submitted and got approved. To interact with a portal you have to physically go to it and get it inside your range ring.

## The colours

A portal's colour tells you who controls it:

- **Grey** is neutral. Nobody owns it. Deploy a single resonator and it's yours.
- **Blue** is Resistance. Your team. These are the ones you build up and defend.
- **Green** is Enlightened. The opposition. Your job is to take these down and turn them blue.
- **Red** is Machina, the AI faction. Covered in the previous op. Treat red as something to clear.

## Levels

Every portal has a **level from 1 to 8**. The level is set by the resonators deployed on it (the next lessons cover those). Higher-level portals are better: they give you better gear when you hack them, they can link further, and they're tougher to knock down.

The level is simply the average of the portal's eight resonator slots. Fill all eight slots with level 8 resonators and you've got a level 8 portal. Leave some empty or use low-level resonators and the level drops accordingly.

One thing worth knowing early: **a single agent can only build a portal up to level 5 on their own.** Getting a portal to levels 6, 7, or 8 takes multiple agents working together, which is one of many reasons knowing your local team matters. A full level <Lvl n={8} /> portal needs eight different level <Lvl n={8} /> agents to deploy on it.

## What you can do at a portal

Depending on whose it is and what you're carrying, you can:

- **Hack** it for items (any portal, any faction).
- **Deploy** resonators and mods to capture or strengthen it (neutral or friendly).
- **Attack** it to bring it down (enemy or Machina).
- **Link** it to another portal (friendly, fully built).

Get to a portal, tap it, and see what lights up. That's the loop. The rest of this op walks through each piece.
