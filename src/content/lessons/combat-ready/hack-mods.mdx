Two mods exist purely to let you hack a portal more, and faster. Together they're what make a **farm**: a portal kitted out so agents can pull gear from it rapidly. Both come in Common, Rare, and Very Rare.

## The normal limits, as a reminder

Without mods, a portal lets you hack it once every **five minutes**, up to **four hacks** before it burns out for four hours. Hack mods loosen both of those limits.

## Multi-hacks (more hacks)

A **multi-hack** raises how many times you can hack a portal before it burns out. Rarer ones add more hacks. A portal with a multi-hack lets everyone squeeze far more out of it before it's spent.

## Heat sinks (faster hacks)

A **heat sink** cuts the cooldown between hacks:

| Heat sink | Cooldown reduction | Time between hacks |
|---|---|---|
| Common | 20% | 4:00 |
| Rare | 50% | 2:30 |
| Very Rare | 70% | 1:30 |

Heat sinks also do something handy the moment you deploy one: for **you specifically**, they **reset the portal's cooldown and burnout timers**, so you can immediately hack again even if you'd just burned it out. That reset is a big part of why they're so good for farming.

## Stacking

For both mod types, if you put more than one on a portal, **only the rarest works at full strength** and the others are half as effective. So the ideal isn't four Very Rares, it's one good one plus cheaper ones to top up.

## How agents actually farm

Put a **multi-hack on first** to raise the hack limit, then a **heat sink** to cut the cooldown and reset the timers, and you can pull a large pile of gear or keys from a single portal in a short time. A good standing farm, a cluster of high-level portals modded like this, is where your team stocks up, and it's worth knowing where your local ones are. Ask in your chat.

One thing to note: hack mods help **everyone**, including the enemy. A heavily modded enemy portal with rare heat sinks is often a sign the opposition is farming there, so it cuts both ways.
