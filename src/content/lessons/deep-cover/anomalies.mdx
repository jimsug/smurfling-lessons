**Anomalies** are the marquee events of Ingress: large, city-based battles where both factions descend on one place and fight for control over a set window. They're organised by Niantic Spatial, they happen in cities all over the world, and a good one is genuinely one of the best experiences the game offers.

{/* VISUAL: agents massed at an anomaly, portals flipping across the city */}

## What happens

An anomaly is a **cross-faction competition**, usually on a Saturday, typically running about **four hours** and divided into phases. The exact rules for anomalies have changed over time, so check in with your local community to find out more.

At set moments the scores are **measured**, and the faction ahead at the end wins. Afterward everyone gathers at an **after-party**, where results are announced, swag is handed out, and (if you care about the fiction) the outcome often feeds into the ongoing Ingress storyline.

These days anomalies run as **seasons**: a run of events across a few months, with a tiered medal for your performance across the season.

## A new agent at an anomaly

You might think an anomaly is no place for a beginner. The opposite is true. Anomalies have **roles for every pace and level**: fast movers capturing portals, static agents holding ground or recharging, operators coordinating from a screen. Your faction will slot you into a team and tell you what's needed. You don't need to be good. You need to show up and follow the plan.

It's also, frankly, the best way to meet your wider community. Agents travel from all over for these, and the social side (the meals, the swag trading, the after-party) is half the point.

## If you go

Some practical advice for your first one:

- **Max out your inventory beforehand.** You'll burn through gear fast.
- **Bring a battery pack.** Four hours of solid play will flatten your phone.
- **Wear comfortable shoes and bring water.** You'll walk a lot.
- **Tell your faction's leadership you're coming.** You don't need to register to earn the onsite participation medal, that comes from just turning up and playing, but your team will want to slot you into the plan (and some ticketed side-ops need their own tickets).

There might not be one in your area, but if an anomaly comes anywhere near you, go. Tell your local team you're keen and they'll look after you. It's the kind of day that turns a casual player into a committed one.
