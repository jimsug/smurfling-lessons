Your inventory has a hard cap of **2,000 items**, and if you do much linking you'll discover that keys eat into that fast. **Key lockers** are the fix.

## What they do

A key locker stores keys, and crucially, **keys held in a locker don't count toward your 2,000-item limit**. So a locker effectively expands how much you can carry, as long as the extra is keys.

They're bought from the in-game store with **CMU** (Chaotic Matter Units, the in-game currency). The best value is the **bundle of five**, which between them lets you stash an extra 500 keys outside your inventory cap. For an active fielder, that's the difference between hoarding keys for a big operation and constantly hitting the wall.

## The one limitation

You **can't use a key while it's locked away**. A locked key is in storage, not in hand, so before you can link with it you have to pull it back out of the locker into your inventory. In practice this means you locker the keys you're saving for later, and keep the ones you're about to use loose.

## Do you need one yet?

Not on day one. Key lockers matter once you're linking and fielding seriously and your inventory starts filling with keys you want to keep. When you notice keys crowding out everything else, that's the signal to grab a locker bundle.

It's one of the more genuinely useful things to spend CMU on, since it permanently raises your effective carrying capacity. Most committed linkers consider the bundle of five an early priority once they've got the currency for it.
