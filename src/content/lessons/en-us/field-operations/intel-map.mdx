Your scanner only shows the area right around you. The **Intel Map** shows the whole world. It lives at **intel.ingress.com**, you sign in with your Ingress account, and it's one of the most useful tools you've got.

{/* VISUAL: a screenshot of the Intel Map at intel.ingress.com showing portals, links and fields */}

## What it shows

The Intel Map is a view-only map of the live game. You can see:

- **Portals**, their owners, and their levels.
- **Links and fields** across the area. Links come in three colors (blue, green, and red for Machina), but fields only in blue and green, since Machina makes links but doesn't create fields.
- The wider state of play well beyond your scanner's range.

You can't take field actions from it. No hacking, no deploying, no linking. What you *can* do, besides look, is **read comms**, and post to them once you reach level 8, so it doubles as a way to chat with your team from a desktop while you plan. Think of it as the map table back at base while your scanner is the kit you carry in the field.

## What it's good for

A few things the Intel Map makes much easier:

- **Planning fields.** Before you head out, you can see which portals you'd need and whether any existing links are in the way. Since links can't cross, spotting a blocker in advance saves a wasted trip.
- **Reading the area.** You can see where the Resistance is strong, where the Enlightened are dug in, and where the gaps are.
- **Finding work.** Spot an enemy field that needs taking down, or a cluster of neutral portals ripe for capture.

## Two practical notes

- The Intel Map is also where **iPhone agents redeem passcodes**, covered in the passcode lesson.
- There are third-party tools that add features on top of the Intel Map, and your local community will have opinions on what's worth using. That's a conversation to have with them rather than something to set up on day one. Ask in your chat when you're ready.

Get familiar with intel.ingress.com early. A few minutes studying the map before you go out turns a wander into a plan.
