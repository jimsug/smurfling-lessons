**Recursion** is something you won't touch for a long while, but it's worth knowing what it is so it makes sense when you see it on other agents.

## What it is

When an agent reaches level 16, the top of the ladder, they can choose to **recurse**: reset their AP back to zero and return to level 1, while keeping all their badges and stats. It's a prestige system, the same idea as in plenty of other games. You earned your way to the top, and now you go again from the bottom with something to show for it.

## What you keep

Recursing doesn't cost you your achievements:

- **All your badges stay**, with an added decoration showing how many times you've earned each one.
- **Your lifetime AP** is tracked separately. After recursing you have two AP numbers: your current AP (climbing again from zero) and your lifetime total, which only ever goes up.
- **A recursion icon** appears next to your name, a small wing marker that shows everywhere your name appears. It's how you spot a recursed agent at a glance.

## Why agents do it

Leveling stops at 16, so for a long-term player recursion is a way to keep the climb going and to wear the badge of having done it. Some agents recurse multiple times. It's entirely optional, and plenty of agents never bother. There's no wrong choice here.

For now, file it away. If you see an agent with wings by their name, that's someone who hit the ceiling and decided to start the journey again. You've got a fair bit of game ahead of you before that's a decision you'll face.
