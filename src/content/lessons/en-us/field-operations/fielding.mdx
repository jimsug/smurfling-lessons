This is the heart of the territory game. Link three of your portals together so they form a triangle, and the triangle fills in with color. That's a **control field**, and fields are how the Resistance actually scores against the Enlightened.

{/* VISUAL: three portals linked into a triangle, filled in as a control field */}

## How a field works

A field needs three portals, each linked to the other two. Once the third link closes the triangle, the field appears. It earns a flat **1,250 AP** the moment it's created, on top of the AP for the three links.

Every field also claims <Term id="mind-unit">Mind Units (MU)</Term>. MU roughly represents the number of people living under the area the field covers. This is the score that matters at a global level: the running tally of blue MU versus green MU is the scoreboard the whole game is fought over.

## Big fields aren't always better

Here's a thing new agents get wrong. A field's **AP is the same 1,250 regardless of size**, so a tiny field over a car park pays exactly as much AP as a giant one. What changes with size is **MU**, and MU depends on population, not area.

So a small field over a dense city block can be worth more MU than an enormous field over empty countryside. If you're playing for AP, lots of small fields beat one big one. If you're playing for MU, you want your fields over where people actually live.

## Layering

You don't have to stop at one field over an area. **Layering** means building multiple fields stacked over the same ground, each one claiming its own MU. The classic technique uses two portals as **anchors** and links a row of portals back to them, throwing many overlapping fields across the same space.

Layering is where fielding gets genuinely strategic, and it's usually a team effort. You don't need to master it as a new agent. Just know that when you see a dense stack of blue fields over your city, someone planned that, and it's the kind of thing your local team does together.

## The catch to remember

A field limits linking underneath it. Once an area is fielded, portals trapped under it can only throw **short links, under 2,000 meters**, until the field comes down. Longer links out are blocked. So if you're relying on a long link from a portal, make it before you close the triangle over it, not after.
