Someone in your local community handed you this link, which means you have
already taken the first step. Welcome aboard. Here is what you have actually
joined.

Ingress is a game played on top of the real world. Your phone is your scanner.
The map on it is the world around you, and scattered across that map are
**portals**: real places, monuments, murals, post offices, churches, oddly
specific bits of public art. To do anything in Ingress, you have to physically
go to these places. There is no playing from the couch. That is the point, and
it is the bit that makes the game worth your time.

## The very short version

The world runs on a made-up energy called **XM** (Exotic Matter). Two human
factions disagree about what to do with it. You have joined the **Resistance**,
the blue team. The other side, the **Enlightened**, are green. There is also a
third, machine-controlled faction shown in red, but ignore that for now. You
will meet it later.

{/* VISUAL: the core loop as a simple cycle diagram, hack -> deploy -> link -> field */}

The basic loop is simple:

- **Hack** portals to collect gear.
- **Deploy** gear to capture portals for the Resistance, turning them blue.
- **Link** your portals together, and link three into a triangle to create a
  **field**.
- Fields cover territory and score points for your faction.

That is the whole game in four lines. Everything else is detail, and we will get
to it.

## What it is not

It is not a game you rush. It rewards getting outside, walking somewhere new,
and knowing the people who play near you. The agents who enjoy Ingress most are
the ones who treat it as a reason to explore and a reason to show up to things,
not a leaderboard to grind alone.

You do not need to understand all of it today. You need to know enough to get
out the door and start hacking. The rest comes with playing, and with asking the
people who recruited you.
