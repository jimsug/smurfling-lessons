Ingress makes you move, but it does not much care how. Agents have invented names for the various ways of getting around, and you will hear all of them.

## Walkgress

Playing on foot. The classic way, and still the best for getting to know an area properly. Portals that are off the beaten track, the ones you only reach by walking, tend to last longer, are harder for the opposition to reach, and are a genuinely good excuse to explore parts of your own city you have never bothered with. A lot of agents will tell you the walking is the part they came for and the game is the excuse.

## Bikegress

Playing on a bike. Fast, covers ground, and reaches places a car cannot. Cyclists are valuable to a team because they can move quickly and still get off-road. At larger events, especially anomalies, a few agents on bikes can do a lot of work in a short time.

## Cargressing

Playing from a car, as a passenger. Useful for covering distance and reaching spread-out portals, and the practical way to play in rural areas where portals are kilometers apart. Ingress can be played from a car, train, bus, tram, boat, or plane. The map does not mind.

## The one rule

**Do not play while driving.** This is the only hard line here. The game has a built-in speed lock that stops you taking actions when you are moving too fast, precisely so that nobody is hacking portals at the wheel. If you are the driver, you are driving. Pull over, or let a passenger play.

Beyond that, mix and match. Most agents walk most of the time, bike when they want to cover more, and get a lift to the far-flung stuff. Use whatever gets you to the portal. Just keep your eyes up and play safe.
