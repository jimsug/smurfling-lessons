We've reached the last lesson, and it's a fitting one to end on, because **missions** are the part of Ingress that most purely rewards getting out and exploring.

## Missions

A **mission** is a curated sequence of tasks, usually visiting or acting on a set of portals in a particular order. The tasks might be hacking, capturing, linking, or just visiting and entering a passphrase. Complete a mission and you earn a unique **medal icon** that sits on your agent profile.

To start one, open Missions from your scanner, browse what's nearby, and pick one. The scanner guides you through each step. Missions are a lovely way to discover an area, since someone has effectively built you a themed walking tour through the interesting parts of it.

## Banners (mosaics)

Here's where it gets satisfying. A **banner**, also called a **mosaic**, is a set of missions whose individual icons are designed to **tile together into a larger picture** on your profile. Complete all the missions in the banner, in order, and the icons line up into artwork: a landmark, a logo, a scene.

Banners are built in rows of **6**, so they come in any multiple of six, from a single row up to big multi-row mosaics. Completing a large one is a proper undertaking, often a full day's walk or more, and a genuine point of pride. (Banners officially adopted by Niantic, like First Saturday's, are capped at 6 or 12.) Events like First Saturday and Mission Days frequently have their own banners to collect.

## Finding and making them

- **Finding banners:** the best tool is **Bannergress** (bannergress.com), a community site that maps banners worldwide so you can find ones near you or plan them into a trip.
- **Making them:** agents at **level 7 or higher** can author their own missions and banners through the Mission Authoring Tool. Creating a good banner for your area is a real contribution, and a creative one.

## A good note to finish on

If anomalies are the intense end of Ingress and farming is the practical end, missions are the **contemplative** end: a reason to walk somewhere new, at your own pace, and come away with both a deeper knowledge of a place and a nice bit of art for your profile.
