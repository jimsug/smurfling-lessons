XM runs out, usually at the worst moment, mid-fight and nowhere near a good cluster. **Power cubes** are bottled XM you hack from portals and save for exactly those moments.

## Standard power cubes

A power cube refills your XM the instant you use it. They come in levels 1 to 8, holding more the higher the level:

| Level | XM |
|---|---|
| <Lvl n={1} /> | 1,000 |
| <Lvl n={2} /> | 2,000 |
| <Lvl n={3} /> | 3,000 |
| <Lvl n={4} /> | 4,000 |
| <Lvl n={5} /> | 5,000 |
| <Lvl n={6} /> | 6,000 |
| <Lvl n={7} /> | 7,000 |
| <Lvl n={8} /> | 8,000 |

To use one, tap it in your inventory and use it, and your XM jumps up. Simple. Keep a stack of them, especially before heading into a fight, so you're never stranded with an empty bar. You can use the cube shortcut under your XM gauge to access them quickly.

Two useful details:

- **Excess XM is wasted.** If you have 1,000 XM to fill and use an 8,000 cube, most of it is lost. So the savvy move is to **spend yourself down first** (remotely recharging your team's portals is a good way) before topping up with a big cube, so none of it goes to waste.
- **Cubes recycle for their full value.** If your inventory is clogged with low-level cubes, recycling them gives you the same XM as using them, so a pile of junk cubes is never truly wasted.

## The hypercube

The **hypercube** is the Very Rare upgrade. Instead of dumping all its XM in one go, it acts as a **backup tank that tops you up automatically** as you spend, until it's empty. A small second bar appears next to your XM.

A few differences from a normal cube:

- You can activate it even when your **XM tank is full**, which a normal cube won't let you do.
- It **doesn't extend your maximum XM**, it just keeps refilling you to your cap as you go. So it can't let you do anything you couldn't otherwise afford, it just saves you reaching for cubes constantly.
- You can only run **one at a time**.

Hypercubes are brilliant for sustained fighting, like clearing a row of defended portals, where you'd otherwise burn through a dozen normal cubes.

(If you read an old guide mentioning Lawson or Circle K power cubes, ignore it. Those sponsored cubes were retired years ago and folded into the hypercube.)
