**Capsules** are storage containers that hold other items. They're how you keep your inventory tidy, move gear between agents, and bundle things up.

## What they do

A standard capsule holds up to **100 items** of any type. They're useful for:

- **Sorting.** Keep your spare resonators in one, your weapons in another, your event swag in a third. You can label them so you know what's in each at a glance.
- **Faster swapping.** Pulling a full set of gear out of a capsule is quicker than hunting through your whole inventory.
- **Trading.** Capsules are how agents pass gear to each other. A higher-level agent can fill a capsule with good gear and hand it to a new agent, which is one of the ways your team will help you get started.

Note that items inside a capsule **still count toward your 2,000 cap**. Capsules organize your inventory, they don't expand it. The one exception is the **key locker** (covered back in field operations), which holds keys outside your cap.

## What capsules no longer do

This is the important bit, because every older guide gets it wrong.

There used to be a special capsule, the **Quantum Capsule** (and before it, the MUFG Capsule), that would slowly **duplicate** the items left inside it. Agents would stuff them with useful gear and let them multiply over time. It was a major way to get rare items.

**That's gone.** Quantum Capsules were retired in early 2023 and converted into ordinary capsules. **Item duplication is no longer a mechanic in Ingress.** If a guide tells you to duplicate gear by leaving it in a Quantum Capsule, that guide is out of date.

What replaced it, in spirit, is the **Kinetic Capsule**, which lets you craft rarer items by walking. That's a different thing entirely, and it's the next lesson.
