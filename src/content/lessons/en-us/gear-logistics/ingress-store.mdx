The **store** is where you can spend currency on items and extras. You never *have* to spend a cent on Ingress, and plenty of agents don't. But a few things in the store are genuinely useful, so it's worth knowing what's there.

## CMU, the currency

The store runs on **CMU**, the in-game currency. You get CMU two ways: buying it with real money, or receiving a monthly grant as a **C.O.R.E.** subscriber (below). Most of what you'd spend it on is convenience and consumables rather than raw power, which is deliberate. Ingress doesn't really let you buy your way to winning.

## What's worth buying

A few things actually earn their keep:

- **Key lockers.** Covered back in field operations. For anyone who links and fields, the bundle of five is one of the best CMU buys there is, since it permanently expands how many keys you can carry.
- **Frackers.** The farming tool from the last lesson. Worth having a few for flash farms.
- **Beacons.** Items you place on a portal to leave a visible mark for a few hours, used to flag a meetup spot or mark an event location. They count toward your inventory while carried.
- **Gear bundles.** The store sells bundles of resonators, weapons, and mods. Handy in a pinch, though you'll mostly gear up by hacking and farming for free.

## C.O.R.E.

**C.O.R.E.** is the optional monthly **subscription**. For a recurring fee it gives you:

- A monthly grant of **CMU** to spend in the store.
- **+500 inventory capacity** (so 2,500 instead of 2,000).
- A monthly exclusive **loadout kit** of gear.
- Various smaller perks, like extra options on the Intel Map.

Whether it's worth it depends entirely on how much you play. A casual agent doesn't need it. A heavy fielder who's always bumping the inventory cap and wants the monthly gear might find it pays for itself. There's no pressure either way, and you lose nothing by skipping it.

## The honest summary

The store is convenience, not advantage. The best gear in the game is hacked, farmed, and crafted for free by getting out and playing. Spend on the store if it makes your play smoother, but never feel you have to. A well-connected agent with an empty wallet will always out-contribute a lone one with a full one.

Oh, and don't forget to check the store daily for a freebie.
