Everything you hack, deploy, and carry lives in your **inventory** (the OPS screen), and it has a hard limit. Managing it well is one of those quiet skills that separates agents who always have what they need from agents who are constantly stuck.

{/* VISUAL: the OPS inventory screen with the item count near the 2,000 cap */}

## The cap

Your inventory holds a maximum of **2,000 items**. That sounds like a lot until you've been hacking for a week. Resonators, weapons, keys, mods, <Term id="power-cube">power cubes</Term>, <Term id="capsule">capsules</Term>, it all counts, and it fills up faster than you'd think.

If you subscribe to **C.O.R.E.** (the paid subscription, covered in the store lesson), your cap goes up by 500, to 2,500. Most agents play perfectly happily without it.

One quirk worth knowing: if you're near the cap and pull a big haul from a glyph hack, you can briefly **overflow past 2,000**. But once you're over, you can't hack again or pick up dropped items until you're back under. So a full inventory effectively benches you.

## Keeping it under control

A few habits keep your inventory healthy:

- **Recycle what you don't need.** Tap items in your inventory and recycle them to turn them into XM. Low-level resonators and weapons you'll never fire are prime candidates. Recycling is also how you turn a pile of junk gear back into useful XM.
- **Use capsules to organize.** Capsules and <Term id="key-locker">key lockers</Term> (both covered next) let you bundle and store gear, and key lockers in particular keep keys out of your main count.
- **Don't hoard.** It's tempting to keep everything. Resist it. A lean inventory full of gear you'll actually use beats a full one clogged with level 1 scraps.

## What to keep

As a rough guide, you always want a healthy stock of: resonators across the levels you can deploy, bursters and Ultra Strikes for attacking, power cubes for XM, keys for portals you're working with, and a few shields. Everything else is negotiable.

Inventory management isn't glamorous, but the first time you're standing at a portal you need to take down with a full inventory and no room to hack more weapons, you'll understand why it matters. Stay lean.
