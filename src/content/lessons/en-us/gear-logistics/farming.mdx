**Farming** is the act of hacking a group of portals hard to stock up on gear or keys. It's how agents fill their inventories before an operation, and it's a very normal part of play. There are three flavors, and your local team will run some version of all of them.

{/* VISUAL: a farm cluster of high-level portals fitted with hack mods, agents hacking together */}

## Standing farms

A **standing farm** is a cluster of portals, usually high level and fitted with **hack mods** (<Term id="multi-hack">multi-hacks</Term> and <Term id="heat-sink">heat sinks</Term>, from the combat op), that's left up so agents can hack it whenever. Because the mods raise the hack limit and cut the cooldown, you can pull a lot from a standing farm in a short visit.

These are the everyday workhorses. Your area almost certainly has a few known standing farms, and finding out where they are is worth a question in your local chat.

## Flash farms

A **flash farm** is an organized, time-boxed event. A group of agents agree to meet at a cluster, build the portals up, mod them heavily, and hack them together for a set window. Often they'll deploy **frackers** (below) and sometimes flip the portals temporarily to keep the other faction from smashing them mid-farm.

Flash farms are a great place to be a new agent. You turn up, hack alongside everyone, and walk away with far more gear than you could gather alone. Watch your local chat for them.

## Walking farms

A **walking farm**, more often just called a **hack run**, is a route rather than a cluster: a string of portals you hack one after another as you walk past. Less a formal farm than the other two, but a pleasant way to top up while you're out and about, and good exercise besides.

## Frackers

A **PortalFracker** is the key tool for serious farming. Deploy one on a portal and, for **10 minutes**, **every item hacked from that portal is duplicated**, for every agent hacking it, not just you. On a well-modded portal in a flash farm, a fracker turns a good haul into a great one.

A few notes: frackers are bought (they're not hacked from portals), deploying one earns AP, and the 10-minute clock is short, so they're timed to coincide with a farm in full swing rather than dropped casually.

## The takeaway

You don't need to organize farms as a new agent. You just need to **show up to them** and know where the standing ones are. A team that farms together is a team that's always geared up, and getting plugged into that is one more reason the community lesson back at the start mattered.
