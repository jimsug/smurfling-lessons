Hacking is how you get almost everything: resonators, weapons, keys, mods, power cubes. You'll do it more than any other action, so it's worth understanding properly.

## The basic hack

Get within range of a portal, tap it, and tap **Hack**. The portal coughs up a handful of items into your inventory. That's it. The level of gear you get scales with the portal's level, so a level 8 portal hands out mostly level 8 items, while a level 1 portal gives you level 1 scraps. If you want good gear, hack good portals.

Hacking is free (no XM cost) and you can hack friendly, enemy, neutral, or Machina portals, though enemy portals give you less and Machina portals are a special case covered earlier.

## The cooldowns

You can't just hack the same portal forever. Two limits apply:

- **Five minutes** between hacks of a neutral or enemy portal, or **three minutes** for a friendly (blue) one.
- **Four hacks** per portal in any four-hour window, after which it "burns out" until the window resets.

These limits can be loosened with certain mods (multi-hacks and heat sinks, covered in the combat op), which is why agents farm gear at portals kitted out with them. For now, hack, wait out the cooldown, hack again.

## The three ways to hack

If you **hold down** the hack button instead of tapping, you get options:

- **Glyph hacking.** A short memory minigame. The scanner flashes a sequence of patterns (glyphs) and you trace them back in order before the timer runs out. Get them right and you earn bonus items and noticeably more AP than a normal hack. It's the most rewarding way to hack and worth learning. Early on you'll fumble it, which is fine.
- **Key hacking.** Biases the hack toward giving you that portal's **key**, which you need for linking. Use this when you're collecting keys for a specific portal.
- **No-key hacking.** The opposite: hack for items but deliberately avoid getting a key. Useful for keeping your inventory clear when you don't need the key and don't want it taking up space.

## The habit to build

Hacking is the rhythm of the game. A good session is often just moving between portals, hacking each as you arrive, glyphing the ones worth the effort, and keeping your inventory stocked. Get into that rhythm and you'll always have the gear you need.
